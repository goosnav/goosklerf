FRONT MATTER
1. What This Book Is
Goosklerf is a modular tabletop card-and-dice game system. The same cards may be used in several ways:
Total War Ń the official flagship mode
Campaign Ń linked fortress battles
Tactical Ń smaller fixed-loadout battles
Lightning Ń fast small-team matches
Skirmish Ń juiced one-off battles
Chessklerf Ń board-based positional mode
Dungeons Ń RPG / campaign / DM mode
This rulebook is written so that:
the text alone is enough to play,
illustrations clarify rather than carry the rules,
easier rule levels can be used first,
the advanced modules can be layered on top.
2. Official Rules Philosophy
Goosklerf II is built around two live stats:
Attack
Hit Points
Complexity comes from:
fortress effects,
items,
consumables,
specials,
scenarios,
optional rules.
3. Rule Priority
When two rules conflict, use this order:
Scenario rule
Card text
Mode rule
Advanced / optional rule
Core rule
Table ruling
If a card or rule instructs an impossible action, do as much of that action as possible and ignore the rest.

PART I Ń COMPONENTS, CARD TYPES, AND TABLE LAYOUT
5. Components
A standard game uses:
Goosklerf cards
One six-sided die per player, minimum
Additional d6s for HP tracking
Optional tokens or markers for control, buffs, rounds, and scenarios
Optional play mat or printed board
6. Card Types
6.1 Entities
Entities are units that fight, occupy fortresses, equip items, and use specials.
Entity color rules
Red border = normal entity
Purple border = rare entity
Entity rules
Only entities can equip and use items.
Entities may attack enemy entities and enemy fortresses when legally able.
Entities are defeated when their HP reaches 0.
Entities choose their targets.
Some entities have Special text.
6.2 Fortresses
Fortresses are structures placed in the suburbs. They house entities and may buff, debuff, or modify the entities inside them.
Fortress color rules
Blue border = normal fortress
Orange border = rare fortress
Fortress rules
Fortresses are placed only in the suburbs.
Fortresses may contain entities.
Fortresses cannot hold or use items.
Fortresses may grant continuous buffs, debuffs, or special effects.
A fortress is destroyed when its HP reaches 0.
If a fortress is destroyed, all entities and items inside it are destroyed with it, unless a rule explicitly saves them.
6.3 Items
Items are attached to entities.
Item color rules
Green border = normal item / consumable
Yellow or gold border = rare item / rare consumable
There are two subtypes:
A. Equipped Items
Remain attached to an entity
Grant continuous buffs, debuffs, or effects
Stay equipped until discarded, transferred, or otherwise removed
B. Consumables
Must still be equipped by an entity to be used
Produce a one-time effect
Are discarded after use unless a card says otherwise
7. Super Cards
7.1 Super Entities
A super entity has two HP dice instead of one.
7.2 Super Fortresses
A super fortress has three HP dice instead of one.
8. Stats
8.1 Attack
Attack is the target number used to hit.
To land a normal hit, roll 1d6:
if the result is equal to or below the attackerŐs modified Attack, the hit lands,
otherwise it misses.
8.2 Hit Points
HP is tracked physically with dice placed on the card.
Standard entities use one die
Super entities use two dice
Standard fortresses use one die
Super fortresses use three dice
For single-die cards, HP ranges from 0 to 6.
For multi-die cards, each die is tracked separately.
8.3 Stat Caps
Unless a card explicitly overrides them:
Attack cannot be reduced below 1 or raised above 5
Single-die HP values cannot be reduced below 0 or raised above 6
GKII entity item capacity is 3
GKII fortress occupant capacity is 3
Battlefield entity cap is 4 per player
If a buff would push a stat above its cap, it is set to the cap instead.
9. Table Zones
9.1 Battlefield
The battlefield is the central combat zone.
Entities on the battlefield can attack enemy battlefield entities
Entities on the battlefield can assault enemy fortresses in the suburbs
Only entities attacking from the battlefield can capture an enemy fortress
9.2 Suburbs
The suburbs are the fortress zones.
Fortresses are placed only in the suburbs
Entities inside fortresses are considered to be in the suburbs
In core play, entities inside fortresses cannot directly attack battlefield entities from inside the fortress
In core play, entities inside fortresses may attack enemy fortresses only if the Fortresses Attacking Fortresses module is active
9.3 Graveyard
Each player has a separate graveyard. Destroyed, defeated, discarded, or spent cards go here unless a rule says otherwise.
9.4 Shop
The Shop is an optional purchase area used only when the Shop module is active.
9.5 Deck and Hand
Each player has:
a deck,
a hand,
a graveyard.
Only the owner may search their deck unless a card says otherwise.
10. Official Table Layout
The printed book should show:
Battlefield in center
Each playerŐs suburbs above or below the battlefield
Shop on one side
Deck, hand, graveyard near each player
Optional round and control trackers at the top edge
PART II Ń CORE RULES
11. Victory Conditions
In PvP, there are three official end conditions.
11.1 Last Man Standing
If only one player or one team has any surviving entities in play, that player or team wins immediately.
11.2 Landlord
If one player or one team controls all fortresses in play, and there are no remaining unplayed fortresses that can legally be added to the game, that player or team wins at the end of the current round if they still control all fortresses.
11.3 Hamlet
If all entities are dead and no player can legally play a new entity, everyone loses immediately.
11.4 Scenario Victory
A scenario may define its own victory condition. Scenario victory overrides normal victory conditions.
12. Control and Occupation
12.1 Control
A player controls a fortress if:
it began the game in their deck and has not been captured, or
they captured it earlier and it remains in play under their control.
12.2 Occupation
A fortress is occupied by whichever player currently has one or more surviving entities inside it.
A player may control a fortress even if it is empty.
12.3 Team Control
In team games, allied fortresses are considered friendly fortresses for movement and occupancy. Teammates may share fortresses, but not entities or items.
13. Deck Construction
13.1 Official Constructed Deck Sizes
The game supports three official deck sizes:
Quick Deck
24 cards
Recommended for Lightning or small Total War games
Standard Deck
36 cards
Recommended official default for Total War
Grand Deck
54 cards
Recommended for long Total War or large Campaign play
13.2 Minimum Composition
For official constructed play:
Quick Deck (24)
minimum 8 entities
minimum 4 fortresses
remainder any items
Standard Deck (36)
minimum 12 entities
minimum 6 fortresses
remainder any items
Grand Deck (54)
minimum 18 entities
minimum 9 fortresses
remainder any items
13.3 Duplicate Limit
Official constructed rule:
maximum 3 copies of a card with the same exact title
If your cards do not have unique titles, treat cards with identical art and text as the same card.
13.4 Casual Distribution Options
Casual play may also begin using one of these methods, inherited from earlier Goosklerf play:
Shuffle & distribute equally
Draft line / Ňdodgeball methodÓ
Custom decks from collection
These remain legal casual setup methods.
14. Setup
14.1 Starting Hand
Each player draws 7 cards at random as their opening hand.
This initial hand is the only required random hand draw in standard PvP.
14.2 Starting Player
Randomly determine the first player by die roll, coin flip, or mutual agreement.
14.3 Turn Order
Players act clockwise unless a scenario says otherwise.
A round ends once every surviving player or team has taken one turn.
14.4 Deck Searching Rule
After setup, Goosklerf uses deliberate deck selection, not random mid-game drawing. This is a core identity rule.
15. Turn Structure
Each turn has five phases:
Card Play
Non-Combat Movement
Combat Declaration and Resolution
Victory Check
End Step / Refill
16. Card Play Phase
16.1 Minimum and Maximum Cards Played
A player must play at least 1 card and at most 3 cards during Card Play if they have at least one legal play available.
If a player has no legal plays available, they may play 0 cards.
In competitive play, if challenged, a player claiming no legal play must reveal their hand.
16.2 What Counts as Playing a Card
A card is ŇplayedÓ when it is used in one of the following legal ways:
placing a fortress into the suburbs
placing an entity onto the battlefield
placing an entity into a friendly fortress
equipping an item to a legal entity
buying a card from the Shop and immediately placing/equipping it legally
16.3 Illegal Purchases
A card may not be bought from the Shop unless it can be legally placed or equipped immediately. Shop purchases count toward the 1Đ3 cards played that turn.
16.4 Legal Placement Rules
A player may place:
Fortresses
only into their own suburbs
only if they have fewer than 3 fortresses in play unless a scenario says otherwise
Entities
onto the battlefield if battlefield entry is legal
into a friendly or allied fortress with room
Items
only onto entities
only if the target entity has room
16.5 Capacity and Limits
Unless legacy rules are active:
max 3 items per entity
max 3 entities per fortress
max 4 battlefield entities per player
If a card effect would create an illegal overflow, the controlling player chooses which legal cards remain and discards the rest.
17. Non-Combat Movement Phase
During Non-Combat Movement, a player may reposition any number of their own entities and transfer any number of their own items, as long as all resulting positions remain legal.
17.1 Legal Entity Movement
Entities may move:
from battlefield to friendly fortress
from friendly fortress to battlefield
from one friendly fortress to another friendly fortress
into allied fortresses in team play
17.2 Item Transfer
During Non-Combat Movement, equipped items may be transferred between that playerŐs own entities if:
both entities are in play,
the receiving entity has room,
the transfer does not violate any card restriction.
Fortresses cannot hold items.
17.3 Battlefield Entry in Core Play
In core play, the battlefield is open. If a player has legal room on the battlefield, they may place or move entities there freely.
17.4 Battlefield Entry with Contested Battlefield Module
If the Contested Battlefield rule is active, use the special battlefield entry rules in that module instead.
18. Combat Declaration
The active player may declare any number of legal combat engagements.
18.1 One Engagement at a Time
Combat engagements resolve sequentially, one at a time.
No second engagement begins until the first one fully ends.
18.2 One Engagement per Entity per Turn
Each entity may participate in only one engagement per turn, unless a card says otherwise.
18.3 Legal Combat Types
The following combat types exist:
Battlefield Skirmish
Battlefield entities attack battlefield entities.
Fortress Assault
Battlefield entities attack an enemy fortress and/or its occupants.
Fortress Barrage
Fortress entities attack an enemy fortress if the Fortresses Attacking Fortresses module is active.
Battlefield Entry Assault
Only used with Contested Battlefield active.
PART III Ń COMBAT RULES
19. Combat Overview
Combat is resolved in alternating rounds.
Attackers act first
Defenders act second
Continue until one side is destroyed, attackers retreat, or a rule ends the engagement
Each entity in the engagement gets one action per combat round unless a special grants more.
20. Actions Available in Combat
On its action, an entity may do one of the following:
make a normal attack
use an equipped consumable
use a special ability
retreat if retreat rules are active
transfer items if Item Swaps are active
parry if Parry rules are active
use another action explicitly granted by a card or scenario
If an entity has already spent its action this round, it may not act again unless a special says otherwise.
21. Normal Attack
21.1 Declare Target
The acting entity chooses one legal target:
one enemy entity in the engagement, or
the enemy fortress if this is a fortress assault or fortress barrage
21.2 Roll to Hit
Roll 1d6.
If the result is equal to or below the acting entityŐs modified Attack, the hit lands.
If the result is above the modified Attack, the attack misses.
21.3 Damage
A normal hit deals 1 damage unless a card, item, special, or module changes the amount.
21.4 Modified Attack
Modified Attack includes:
item buffs,
fortress buffs that currently apply,
scenario modifiers,
legal active effects.
22. Damage and HP
22.1 Applying Damage
When a target takes damage, reduce its HP by that many points.
22.2 Multi-Die HP
For super entities and super fortresses, damage is applied one point at a time across the HP dice in any order chosen by that cardŐs controller unless the card says otherwise.
22.3 Healing
Healing restores lost HP but may not raise a die above 6 unless a card explicitly says so.
22.4 Persistent Damage
HP does not reset after combat in Goosklerf II core play. Damage remains until healed. This is now official canon.
23. Defeat
23.1 Entity Defeat
An entity is defeated when its total HP reaches 0.
Unless another rule says otherwise:
remove the entity from play,
place it in its ownerŐs graveyard,
place all items equipped to it in that same ownerŐs graveyard.
23.2 Fortress Destruction
A fortress is destroyed when its total HP reaches 0.
When a fortress is destroyed:
discard the fortress,
all entities inside it are destroyed,
all items on those entities are destroyed,
ownership of that fortress ends.
A destroyed fortress cannot be captured.
24. Item Ownership Default Rule
In standard Goosklerf II, items return to their ownerŐs graveyard when discarded, destroyed, or lost, unless:
a scenario says otherwise,
the Scavenger rule is active,
a card explicitly overrides this rule.
This closes the old Ňloot everything automaticallyÓ ambiguity while preserving it for special modes.
25. End of Engagement
An engagement ends when one of the following happens:
all defenders in the engagement are defeated,
all attackers in the engagement are defeated,
the attackers retreat successfully,
the target fortress is destroyed,
a scenario ends the engagement.
26. Fortress Assault Resolution
26.1 Battlefield Assault Requirement
Only entities attacking from the battlefield may capture a fortress.
26.2 Valid Targets in a Fortress Assault
In a fortress assault, attacking battlefield entities may target:
the fortress itself, or
defender entities inside it
Defender entities inside the fortress may target attacking entities.
26.3 Capturing a Fortress
If all defenders in a fortress are defeated and the fortress still has at least 1 HP remaining, the surviving attackers may choose one of two outcomes:
A. Occupy It
The attackers capture and occupy the fortress.
Control changes to the attacking player or team
Up to the fortressŐs capacity of surviving attackers move into the fortress
Any additional surviving attackers remain on the battlefield if legal; if not legal, their controller must move them into a legal friendly fortress if possible, otherwise discard excess survivors of their choice
Captured attackers must remain in the fortress until their controllerŐs next Non-Combat Movement phase.
B. Destroy It
Instead of occupying it, the attackers may immediately destroy the empty or cleared fortress.
Discard the fortress
No one controls it
No additional casualties occur unless a card says otherwise
26.4 If All Attackers Also Die
If the last surviving attacker dies before occupation is established, the fortress remains under its prior control if it still has HP remaining.
27. Battlefield Skirmish Resolution
In a battlefield skirmish:
entities may target enemy battlefield entities only,
the engagement ends when one side is defeated or the attackers retreat.
Battlefield skirmishes do not affect fortress ownership directly.
28. Fortress Barrage Resolution
This combat type exists only when the Fortresses Attacking Fortresses module is active.
In a fortress barrage:
the attacking side chooses one attacking fortress with at least one entity inside,
the target is one enemy fortress,
attacking entities in the attacking fortress may target only the target fortress, not its occupants,
defenders in the target fortress may target attackers normally,
if the target fortress is destroyed, all defenders inside it are destroyed,
if the target fortress survives but its occupants die, the attacking side does not automatically capture it because the attack did not come from the battlefield
To capture a fortress, attackers must still assault it from the battlefield.
29. End Step / Refill
At the end of the turn, the active player searches their deck and chooses up to 3 cards to add to hand.
These cards are not playable until that playerŐs next turn
The owner may search the full remaining deck
If fewer than 3 cards remain, take as many as possible
If the deck is empty, no refill occurs
The graveyard is not shuffled back into the deck unless a scenario or card says so
This deliberate refill rule is official.

PART V Ń INTERMEDIATE RULES
31. Retreat
At the beginning of that entityŐs action, an attacker or defender may choose to retreat instead of acting.
31.1 Retreat Procedure
Declare the retreat instead of taking an action.
Mark that entity as retreating.
On the enemyŐs next normal combat round, enemy entities may target the retreating entity instead of other legal targets.
Any attack against a retreating entity still consumes the enemy attackerŐs action normally.
If the retreating entity survives the enemy round, it retreats successfully at the end of that enemy round.
A retreating entity takes no further actions in that engagement.
31.2 Legal Retreat Destinations
A retreating entity must have a legal destination available.
From a Fortress
A defender inside a fortress may retreat:
to a friendly battlefield position if legal, or
to another friendly fortress with space
If Contested Battlefield is active, a fortress defender may not retreat into a battlefield they do not already legally occupy.
From the Battlefield
A battlefield entity may retreat:
to a friendly fortress with space,
or remain on the battlefield only in cases where it was already legally on the battlefield, including battlefield-entry fights under Contested Battlefield.
If no legal retreat destination exists, the entity may not choose retreat.
32. Last Stand
Defenders only.
If a defending entity is reduced to 0 HP, it may immediately take one final action before being discarded.
That final action may be:
a normal attack,
a consumable,
a special,
a parry if legal,
a retreat if a rule explicitly allows it.
After the Last Stand action, discard the entity unless it was revived.
Attackers do not receive Last Stand.
33. Item Swaps
During combat, instead of taking another action, an entity may transfer up to two equipped items to another friendly entity in the same engagement.
the receiving entity must have room,
the acting entity may not attack that round,
items may not be transferred to an enemy,
in team games, Item Swaps may only occur between entities controlled by the same player unless a card says otherwise.
34. Shop
The Shop is optional but is part of the official advanced Total War experience.
34.1 Personal Shop Setup
At the start of the game, each player draws 7 random cards and places them face-up in their personal Shop.
Only that player may buy from that Shop.
34.2 Buying Rule
To buy 1 Shop card:
discard 2 normal cards, or
discard 1 rare card
This arbitrage is legal and intentional.
34.3 What Counts as Normal or Rare
Rare means:
purple entity
orange fortress
yellow/gold item or consumable
All other cards are normal.
34.4 Timing
Shop purchases occur during Card Play and count as one played card.
34.5 No Legal Placement, No Purchase
A purchased Shop card must be placed or equipped immediately. If it cannot be, it may not be purchased.
PART VI Ń ADVANCED RULES
35. Reinforcements
Reinforcements may be used in any engagement.
35.1 Timing
At the end of a sideŐs combat round, that side may call one reinforcement into that engagement.
35.2 Legal Reinforcement Source
A reinforcement must come from a legal non-engaged zone the player already occupies, such as:
a friendly fortress,
the battlefield,
an allied fortress in team play.
35.3 Entering the Engagement
A reinforcing entity:
enters the engagement immediately,
may be targeted right away,
may not act until the next round,
remains in the zone it reinforced into after combat if it survives
Reinforcements do not Ňsnap backÓ to their origin.
36. Parrying
Parrying preserves your current retroactive design.
Instead of attacking, an entity may choose to parry a single normal attack that landed on the previous enemy turn.
36.1 Procedure
Declare which previous normal hit is being parried.
Roll a normal attack roll using the parrying entityŐs current modified Attack.
If the roll hits, the previously landed normal hit is voided.
Restore the 1 HP lost from that previous hit.
36.2 Restrictions
only one previous normal hit may be parried per parry action
specials cannot be parried
consumable effects cannot be parried
a failed parry still consumes the action
after a parry attempt, that entity cannot attack further that round
37. Contested Battlefield
This module changes how battlefield entry works.
37.1 Core Concept
The battlefield is no longer a freely shared deployment zone.
37.2 Initial Control
The first player to place an entity on the battlefield establishes battlefield presence.
37.3 Entering Without Presence
If a player has no entities on the battlefield, they may not simply place or move one there. They must fight their way onto it.
37.4 Battlefield Entry Assault
To gain battlefield presence:
move an entity out of a friendly fortress,
declare an assault against the largest hostile battlefield force,
resolve that engagement normally.
If forces are tied in size and total HP, the attacker chooses which force to assault.
37.5 Securing Presence
A player secures battlefield presence if:
they defeat at least one enemy battlefield entity and still have a survivor, or
they launched a legal battlefield-entry assault and then successfully retreated, leaving surviving attackers on the battlefield
Once presence is established, that player may place or move further entities to the battlefield normally while they still have at least one battlefield entity there.
38. Supercharged Attack
Instead of a normal attack, an entity may declare a supercharged attack.
38.1 Procedure
Roll 3d6 instead of 1d6.
38.2 Result
If all three dice hit, deal 2 damage
If any one die misses, deal 0 damage and the attacking entity takes 1 self-damage
This attack still consumes the entityŐs action.
39. Scavenger
If a friendly entity dies during an engagement, allied entities in that same engagement may immediately equip its items if they have room.
items already equipped by living entities may not be transferred to the dead entity
if no entity in that engagement has room, excess items are discarded
in team games, ŇalliedÓ means same team only if team-sharing is agreed before play; otherwise same player only
40. Fortresses Attacking Fortresses
Use the Fortress Barrage combat type from Section 28.
Optional Mass Barrage Variant
If all players agree before the game begins, this module may be upgraded into Mass Barrage:
when one fortress barrage begins, all friendly and enemy fortresses with legal attacking entities are considered part of the same barrage
this creates one giant fortress engagement
This variant is intentionally more chaotic and is not the recommended official default.
PART VII Ń OPTIONAL / CHAOS / PARTY MODULES
41. Timed Turns
Recommended only for party play or slow groups.
Non-combat turn limit: 60 seconds
Combat decisions: 15 seconds per acting entity
If the timer expires, the player must immediately proceed to the next phase or forfeit the current entityŐs action
42. Card Shield
An entity may flip an equipped item face down to turn it into a shield card.
face-down shield items provide no buffs and cannot be used
when that entity would take 1 damage, it may discard one shield card instead
each shield card prevents 1 damage
43. High Risk, High Reward
Instead of a normal attack, an entity may declare a high-risk strike.
it must roll an exact 1
if it does, it deals 2 damage
if it fails, it deals 0
44. Community Shop
Instead of personal Shops, all players contribute their Shop cards to one shared face-up market. Any player may buy from it.
45. IRL / Chaos Cards
If you print or play cards that require real-life actions, dares, races, contests, drawing games, or other off-table behavior, they should be labeled clearly as Chaos Cards and may be used only if all players consent before the game begins.
PART VIII Ń TEAM PLAY RULES
46. Teams
Goosklerf may be played free-for-all or in teams.
47. Team Fortresses
Teammates may:
occupy each otherŐs fortresses,
move through each otherŐs fortresses,
reinforce each otherŐs fortresses.
48. Team Restrictions
Teammates may not:
transfer ownership of entities,
transfer item ownership,
equip items to allied entities unless a card explicitly allows it.
49. Team Victory
A team wins if:
all non-team entities are eliminated,
or the team controls all fortresses under Landlord rules.
If Hamlet occurs, all teams lose.

55. CAMPAIGN MODE
Campaign Mode links multiple battles together.
55.1 Core Idea
You fight through a chain of fortresses called a battlepath.
55.2 Competitive Campaign
Each player or team advances through the same number of battlepath levels. The winner is the player or team with the highest campaign score when all battlepaths are cleared or all parties are eliminated.
55.3 Co-op Campaign
All players share one strike force and attempt to clear the full battlepath.
55.4 Persistent Rules
Across campaign battles:
surviving entities persist,
equipped items persist,
fortress trophies persist,
HP may be restored only between missions if the scenario says so, by item, or by rest rules chosen before the campaign begins.
56. SINGLEPLAYER CAMPAIGN Ń OFFICIAL SOLO MODE
This preserves your battlepath / trophy / shop / score structure while making the enemy logic cleaner.
56.1 Setup
Separate your cards into:
fortresses
entities
items
Shuffle each pile separately.
Build the battlepath face down:
Level 1: 1 fortress + 1 entity
Level 2: 1 fortress + 1 entity with 1 item
Level 3: 1 fortress + 2 entities with 1 item each
Level 4: 1 fortress + 3 entities with 1 item each
Level 5: 1 fortress + 3 entities with 2 items each
Level 6: 1 fortress + 3 entities with 3 items each
Level 7+: 2 fortresses + 3 entities per fortress with 3 items each
Choose 3 entities for your strike force.
Draw 3 random items and equip them as desired.
Shuffle the remaining entities and items together.
Reveal 7 cards into the Shop.
56.2 Your Turn
You attack the next fortress on the battlepath.
Flip that level face up and resolve it as a fortress assault.
56.3 Enemy AI
Enemy entities use the following priority:
If an enemy has a legal healing consumable and is at half HP or lower, use it on a roll of 1Đ2 on 1d6.
If an enemy has a legal damage consumable and at least one target, use it on a roll of 1Đ2 on 1d6.
Otherwise perform a normal attack.
Target priority:
lowest current HP target
if tied, highest Attack target
if still tied, choose randomly
Enemy AI does not use advanced strategic combos unless a scenario says otherwise.
56.4 Post-Battle
If you defeat a fortress:
add the fortress to your trophy pile,
confiscate any remaining enemy items,
equip or store those items up to legal capacity,
discard excess items,
then buy from the Shop using items and/or fortress trophies
56.5 Shop Currency in Solo
2 normal cards = 1 Shop card
1 rare card = 1 Shop card
If you buy an entity, it joins your strike force roster.
56.6 Losing
You lose when all your entities are dead.
56.7 Scoring
each level cleared: 100 points
each trophy fortress not spent: 50 points
each surviving entity: 25 points
each remaining item: 10 points
Optional rarity scoring variant:
super fortress = 10
rare fortress = 7
normal fortress = 5
super entity = 5
rare entity = 3
normal entity = 1

PART XI Ń DUNGEONS
This mode is fast, lightweight, and intentionally flexible. It is built to let the same cards become a campaign RPG. It also matches your Ňpage-flip / Dr. Seuss / invent the worldÓ concept and your notes on levels, variable damage, squire rules, and future quest books.
62. DUNGEONS OVERVIEW
One player is the Dungeon Master.
All other players are Adventurers.
The DM may:
use a Dr. Seuss book or any illustrated book as a scene engine,
invent rooms and objectives,
run a prepared Goosklerf campaign,
use the automated tables below.
63. Character Creation
Each adventurer:
Draws or selects 3 entities
Chooses 1 as their starting character
Draws 2 items and 1 consumable
Equips legal starting items
Begins at Level 1
Your entity card is your base character sheet.
64. Core Dungeons Rules
64.1 Non-Combat Checks
When a character attempts something risky outside combat, the DM sets a difficulty:
Easy = 5
Normal = 4
Hard = 3
Extreme = 2
Roll 1d6.
If the result is equal to or below the difficulty, the action succeeds.
Items, specials, and scenario logic may modify difficulty by ±1.
64.2 Combat
Combat uses normal Goosklerf attack rules, but the DM controls how much damage a successful hit deals.
Default recommendation:
weak hit = 1 damage
strong hit = 2 damage
boss or critical hit = 3 damage
64.3 Variable Damage Rule
Optional official Dungeons damage:
After a successful hit, roll 1d6 again.
1Đ3 = 1 damage
4Đ5 = 2 damage
6 = 3 damage
64.4 Enemy HP
The DM may use normal card HP, buffed HP, or scenario HP.
65. Leveling
Each character gains 1 level after each act or major scenario.
Level 1
Base character only
Level 2
+1 maximum HP
Level 3
Once per session, reroll one failed roll
Level 4
+1 Attack, to a maximum of 5
Level 5
Gain a Squire Slot
Level 6
+1 maximum HP
Level 7
Gain one additional item slot in Dungeons mode only
Level 8+
Alternate between:
+1 HP
one reroll per session
one custom feat agreed with DM
66. Squire Rule
At Level 5, a player may assign one extra entity as a squire.
the squire is not a full second character
the squire may hold 1 item
when the hero would take damage, the player may assign that damage to the squire first
if the squire dies, discard it normally

78. Defense Conversion
When a GKI card references Defense in GKII play:
treat that Defense value as Attack when the entity is defending
If a GKI fortress buffs Defense on a GKII entity:
buff that entityŐs Attack instead
79. Capacity Conversion
For pure GKII cards:
entity capacity = 3 items
fortress capacity = 3 entities
For GKI cards:
printed capacity still applies
If capacity changes mid-battle:
the controlling player immediately discards excess items or excess entities of their choice
there is no overflow zone

If a legacy item buffs both Attack and Defense:

PART XV Ń FAQ / EDGE CASES
83. Edge Case Rulings
Q1. What if I must play 1 card but no legal play exists?
You play 0 cards and continue your turn.
Q2. What if my deck is empty?
You do not refill.
Q3. What if a captured fortress would exceed its capacity?
Choose legal occupants up to capacity. Move excess survivors to legal battlefield or friendly fortress spaces if possible. Discard any remaining illegal excess.
Q4. What if my item is removed and my HP was above the new legal maximum?
Reduce current HP to the new legal maximum immediately.
Q5. What if both sides are destroyed in the same engagement?
If the fortress also survives and no attacker survives, it remains under defender control.
If the fortress was destroyed, it is gone.
If the battlefield engagement wipes both sides, that engagement ends with no surviving winner.
Q6. Can I buy a Shop card into my hand for later?
No. Officially, Shop purchases must be placed or equipped immediately.
Q7. Do teammates share item pools?
No.
Q8. Can I move items through a fortress?
No. Fortresses cannot hold items.
Q9. Can I heal above printed HP?
Not unless a card explicitly says so.
Q10. Can I attack allies?
Not unless a scenario or card explicitly permits it.
